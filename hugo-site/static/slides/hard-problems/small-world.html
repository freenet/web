Small-World Routing

  • Problem: Find data without central servers
  • Solution: Each peer has a "location" (0.0-1.0)
  • Connect to near neighbors + random shortcuts
  • Result: O(log n) hops to any data
  • Scales to millions of peers
How do you find data without a central index? Every peer gets a location between 0 and 1. Data also gets a location based on its key hash. Peers connect to nearby neighbors AND random long-range shortcuts. This creates a "small world" - you can reach anyone in logarithmic hops. Same principle that makes "six degrees of separation" work in social networks.
