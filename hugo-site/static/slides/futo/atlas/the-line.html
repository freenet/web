The Line Atlas Draws

The network guarantees

Integrity

Every entry is signed and verifiable. Any node can host and serve the index; nobody has to trust the server.

The edge owns

Judgment

Describing, ranking, deciding what to trust — all of it lives outside the contract, in programs anyone can rewrite.

The network proves what's real. It never decides what's good.

That deliberate split is why discovery here needs no gatekeeper.

  • This is the heart of the section, and it generalizes to every app.
  • The contract's whole job is integrity: is this entry signed by an authorized key, and is it well-formed? It has no opinion about what a page means or how good it is.
  • Everything subjective -- who describes a page, how results are ranked, whose entries you trust -- lives at the edge, outside the network, in ordinary programs anyone can swap out.
  • The one-liner: the network proves what's real, it never decides what's good.
  • Why it matters: because judgment was never put on the network, pluralizing it later isn't a rewrite -- it's just running more programs at the edge. That's the next slide.
