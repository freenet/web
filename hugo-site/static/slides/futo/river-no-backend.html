Room contract on Freenet
  • No River company server
  • No account or phone number
  • The room lives on the network
  • Popular rooms get more available as more peers serve them
  • River isn't interesting because chat is new. It's interesting because the room is network-hosted application state.
  • In a conventional app the company's servers are the room -- the state and the rules both live there.
  • In River the room is a contract on Freenet. Your peer talks to the network, not to a River company that doesn't exist.
  • There's no account and no phone number because there's no service to hold an account against.
  • Last point ties to the hosting slide later: popularity is meant to become capacity, not a server bill.
