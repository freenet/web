Services Without Servers

Every app runs on someone's servers.

your data • your messages • what you're allowed to see

Freenet has no server to own.

no client, no server • just peers

No one runs it.

no subpoena • no takedown • no back door

SAY (cues):

  • The "what is Freenet" slide, for newcomers.
  • Beat 1: whoever owns the server owns all three, your data, your messages, what you see.
  • Beat 2: "server" is one half of client-server. No split here. Peers store and serve, together they're the backend.
  • Connect to the last slide: all of it works by pressuring whoever runs the service. On Freenet, no one runs it.
  • Beat 3: so no one to subpoena, block, or pressure.
  • Land: "services without servers." The rest of the talk is the same idea, a different service each time.

BACKUP (honest scope, say if useful):

  • Not the only approach. Tor, mesh, Nostr attack pieces of this. Freenet's lane is removing the platform lever for general apps.
  • Not a silver bullet. It doesn't beat a mandate baked into your device, a block of the wire itself, or coercion of someone already identified. It removes the cheap, invisible lever being reached for now.
