Rules in Code, Not a Company

Every app needs rules: who can post, what's valid, who owns what. On Freenet those rules live in code anyone can verify, not on a company's server.

contract = the rules, as WebAssembly code (its key is a hash of that code)
state = the app's data, and every change is signed
the contract decides → accept or reject each change, with no server in the loop

And your keys never leave your device: delegates hold them locally, so no one else can act as you.

SAY (cues):

  • The "no platform" payoff: every app has rules, and normally a company's server enforces them.
  • On Freenet the rules ARE the contract: WebAssembly code, and the key is a hash of that code, so the rules are baked into the address.
  • State is the app's data. Every change is signed, and the contract code accepts or rejects it. No server in the loop.
  • Delegates keep your keys and private data on your own machine. The network never sees them.
  • That's what makes private rooms and Mail possible, and why there's no company to run.
